Change padding values

Padding is the space between the content of an element and its border. When you increase padding, the content moves further inward, creating more space inside the box.

For example, a padding of 2px gives very little space, keeping the text close to the border. At 5px, thereâ€™s a small but noticeable gap, while 10px makes the text look comfortably spaced.

As the padding increases to 15px or 20px, the content gains more breathing room, improving readability. With 25px and 30px, the box starts to look spacious and balanced.

At 35px and 40px, the inner space becomes quite prominent, adding a clean, open feel. Finally, with 50px, the content appears deeply inset, giving a centered, airy appearance inside the box.
